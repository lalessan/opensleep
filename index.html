Why I Started OpenSleep

My daughter Luna slept well—until six months. Suddenly, she was waking up multiple times a night, and getting her back to sleep was a struggle.

Like many exhausted parents, I searched everywhere—books, Facebook groups, Reddit, baby sleep websites. One common theme? Daytime sleep affects night wakings. So, I tried to find out how much she should nap at 6 months old.

That’s when I hit a wall. Even trusted sources didn’t agree:

  • Huckleberry: 2.5–3.5 hours (source)
  • Napper: 4 hours (source)
  • Taking Cara Babies: 3–4 hours (source)

Okay… but what’s the reality? How many babies nap 2.5 hours? How many nap 4? And what about those unicorn babies who sleep through the night—how much do they actually nap?

I thought there would be something like growth charts, but for baby sleep—-based on real-world data.

Instead, I found small studies and expert opinions. No clear distributions based on representative data.

And beyond that, I wanted to see patterns. Are longer/shorter naps associated to more night wakings? I couldn’t find answers—because the data simply isn’t out there.

That’s when it hit me: Millions of parents track their baby’s sleep.

Why isn’t this data just shown somewhere?

Don’t Sleep Apps Already Have This Data?

Yes—but they keep it to themselves.

Apps like Huckleberry and Napper collect huge amounts of baby sleep data, but parents never get to see:

  • How real babies sleep across different ages
  • How your baby’s sleep compares to others
  • Patterns—like how the length of naps relates with night wakings

We track our babies' sleep. Shouldn’t we have access to the big picture?

What We Can Build Together

If you’re here, you’re probably part of an online community of parents who swap advice, share experiences, and try to figure out baby sleep together.

What if we pooled our data to build something useful for everyone?

I’ve designed the OpenSleep data collection to uncover real patterns in baby sleep. I’m inviting every parent who tracks sleep to contribute.

When we reach 1,000 contributors, I’ll compile and share:

  • An open dataset that parents, researchers, and doctors can access and use.
  • A free online tool where you can compare your baby’s sleep to a large sample of other babies.
  • New scientific research that helps us understand baby sleep like never before.
How Your Data is Protected

I take privacy seriously and ensure that all data is handled securely and ethically.

  • The OpenSleep data collection complies with GDPR and other applicable data protection laws.
  • Your data is processed securely and will be de-identified before sharing.
  • Your data will never be used for commercial purposes.
  • You have the right to access, correct, or request the deletion of your data at any time.
