Why Baby Sleep is Still a Mystery

One day, when Luna was six months old, her nightsleep went crap.

How could I help her? How much daytime sleep she should be getting? Like many parents, I read books, scrolled through Reddit threads, and tried to make sense of all the conflicting advice. Here’s what I found:

  • Huckleberry: 2.5–3.5 hours (source)
  • Napper: 4 hours (source)
  • Taking cara babies: 3-4 hours (source)

All seemed reasonable. But why the difference?

I would have liked to know how many babies sleep 2 hours and how many sleep 4 hours and what correlates with night time sleep

I have soon realized that these numbers I thought there must be a large study showing how much babies actually sleep at different ages, right? just like the charts doctors use for height and weight. But after searching, I found nothing. No real numbers, just people and expert opinions and small studies.

I just wanted answers: Is my baby sleeping enough? Too much? Are all these night wakes normal?

I am not alone

When I had my daughter Luna, I became obsessed with baby sleep.

Like many parents, I asked advices to my friends, I read books (e.g. a great one!), scrolled through sub-Reddits (e.g. this one), and tried to make sense of all the conflicting advice.

I wasn’t alone, and you are not alone 😂. Every parenting forum I visited was full of parents asking the same questions:

"My baby is five months old and only naps two hours a day—is that normal?"

"How much should a seven-month-old sleep at night?"

"My nine-month-old wakes up four times a night—is anyone else dealing with this?"

That’s when it hit me: Millions of parents track their baby’s sleep.

Why isn’t this data just shown somewhere?

Don’t Sleep Apps Already Have This Data?

Yes, but they keep it from themselves don’t share it with us.

Apps like Huckleberry and Napper collect large amounts of baby sleep data, but parents don’t get to see:

  • Real distributions of baby sleep across different ages
  • Comparisons to other babies in a similar stage (e.g. 70% of babies behave [like this..])
What We Can Build Together

If you’re here, you’re probably part of an online community of parents who swap advice, share experiences, and try to figure out baby sleep together.

What if we pooled our data to build something useful for everyone?

As a researcher in behavioral data science, I’ve designed the OpenSleep data collection to uncover real patterns in baby sleep. I’m inviting every parent who tracks sleep to contribute.

When we reach 1,000 contributors, I’ll compile and share:

  • An open (for everyone) dataset that parents, researchers, and doctors can use and access!
  • A free online tool where you can compare your baby’s sleep to a large sample of real babies
